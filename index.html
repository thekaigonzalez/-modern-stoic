What is Stoicism?

Stoicism is an ancient philosophy that originated in Greece around the 3rd century BC. It teaches that the key to a good life is achieving inner tranquility and virtue by focusing on what we can control and accepting what we cannot."

"Meditations" by Marcus Aurelius

"Meditations" is a series of personal writings by the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius, who ruled from 161 to 180 AD. It is considered one of the most influential works of Stoic philosophy. The book is essentially a collection of Marcus Aurelius' thoughts, reflections, and philosophical musings.

The key principles of Stoicism:

1. Focus on the present moment: Embrace the "here and now" and let go of anxieties about the past or the future.
2. Acceptance of the inevitable: Acknowledge that some things are beyond our control, and instead, focus on how we respond to them.
3. Virtue is the highest good: Strive to cultivate virtues such as wisdom, courage, justice, and temperance, as they lead to a fulfilling life.
4. Practice self-discipline: Train yourself to avoid impulsive reactions and develop resilience in the face of challenges.


Who created Stoicism?

Stoicism was founded by Zeno of Citium, a Greek philosopher, around 300 BC in Athens, Greece. Zeno was influenced by earlier philosophers like Socrates, as well as the teachings of Cynicism. He established the Stoic school of philosophy, which gained popularity and became one of the most influential philosophical movements in ancient Greece and later in Rome.
The Stoic philosophy was further developed and refined by other notable philosophers such as Cleanthes, Chrysippus, and later, Seneca, Epictetus, and the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius. Throughout its history, Stoicism evolved and adapted, leaving a lasting impact on Western philosophy and ethics.

This website will be focusing on Marcus Aurelius' impact on Stoicism solely, but we will provide links to other sites that also talk about other philosophical opinions on Stoicism and how it was impacted.

Marcus Aurelius' Reign

Marcus Aurelius was a Roman Emperor who reigned from 161 AD to 180 AD. He was born on April 26, 121 AD, in Rome. Marcus Aurelius is best known for his philosophical writings, especially "Meditations," which remains a significant work of Stoic philosophy.
